This article is about the 2021 video game. For the 2004 video game, see Hitman: Contracts.

Hitman 3 (stylized as HITMAN™ III) is the 8th and most recent major installment into the Hitman video game series, and serves as the conclusion to the World of Assassination trilogy. It was announced on June 11, 2020 and was released on January 20, 2021.[1] HITMAN™ III is the first game in the Hitman series to be self-published by IO Interactive.[2]
It launched with 6 new locations to explore, and owners of HITMAN™ and HITMAN™2 are be able to 'import' locations introduced in those games into HITMAN™ III. Additionally, HITMAN™ 2 players are given the option to carry over unlocks and progression into HITMAN™ 3.
With the Freelancer update on January 26, 2023 HITMAN III was rebranded to HITMAN: World of Assassination on all platforms, and access passes for HITMAN and HITMAN 2 Standard content were granted to all players, creating a single point of entry for the trilogy.

GamePlay Overview

In HITMAN™ 3, players once again take control of Agent 47 in order to fulfil assassination missions. Missions take place in dynamic locations across the globe, where players can utilize the sandbox nature of the maps to their advantage in order to achieve their goal in any way they see fit. From an assortment of weapons, environmental hazards, distractions and tactics, creative use of the world is at your disposal, along with the return of opportunities and hints from the previous game which aid players into seeing some of the more spectacular assassination methods. As with the previous game in the series, in addition to the standard single-player experience, players can also complete contracts in the Sniper Assassin game mode.

New features that come with HITMAN™ 3 include keypads that are on specific locked doors in the levels (requiring the knowledge of the four-digit code to pass), Permanent shortcuts are also featured (in ladder and door forms), and can be unlocked to cut down on map travel time, and reward player exploration in the process.

As with HITMAN™ and HITMAN™ 2, the game also features Elusive targets; seven of them as of writing that tie into the Seven Deadly Sins DLC that released over seven months of 2021. Beginning in 2022, Elusive Target Arcade was introduced to let players play specific Elusive Targets in an Escalation Contracts-esque fashion, with optional complications, and get rewarded with a unique suit or weapon.

Progression

Progression is centered around mastering locations. As players level up the Mastery track for each location, they unlock starting locations, agency pickups, and new items that give options to take on each mission in new ways. Progress can be achieved by either completing Challenges or earning Performance XP.

Performance XP acts as a second avenue to accumulate experience points. It awards a small amount of XP for doing moment-to-moment actions during a playthrough, like taking someone out silently, hiding a body, or killing a target. By default, Performance XP will be displayed, but this can be toggled on or off in Settings.

A Player Profile levels up according to total XP earned. Player badges are the visual representation of the profile level. Both Mastery and Performance XP are funnelled into the Player Profile as well as the Mastery Track, levelling both up as players progress through the game. Outside of those two systems, items can be unlocked by completing specific Challenges.

Both XP systems also work on locations imported from HITMAN™ and HITMAN™ 2 if players owned the specific Access Passes or used location importing. Mastery tracks for the Legacy locations have been tweaked to give a more directed progression of unlocks.

Difficulty

There are three difficulty levels: Casual, Professional, and Master.

  • Casual is for new players. It gives players an easier way to experiment with the world without fear of failing. Security Cameras have been removed in Casual, and the AI is much more forgiving of combat.

  • Professional (default option). It gives players a feeling of being a true assassin, using all of the game's intricate mechanics to assassinate targets. Cameras are now present in the level, the AI is smarter, and gun fights are considerably more risky to partake in.

  • Master is for the masters of assassinations; those who know the map inside out, and know every trick in the book. Combat is extremely hard and risky, NPCs will be more aware of their surroundings, there are more cameras and enforcers, making stealth gameplay a greater challenge.

As with the progression system, all of difficulty levels can be selected on the Legacy locations within HITMAN 3. Some unlocks can only be earned by completing Challenges, and in an inversion of the previous entries, Hitman 3 does not make a distinction on what difficulty you complete challenges on; especially applicable to The Classics.
